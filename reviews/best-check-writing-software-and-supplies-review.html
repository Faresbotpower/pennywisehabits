Buying Guide: What to Look for in Check Writing Software and Supplies

Software Considerations

Integration Capabilities

Consider how well the check writing software integrates with your existing accounting system, bank accounts, and business processes. Software that can import data from your accounting system or directly connect to your bank will save significant time and reduce errors.

Security Features

Look for software that offers robust security features including password protection, user access controls, check numbering systems, and fraud prevention measures. The ability to void checks and maintain audit trails is crucial for business compliance.

Ease of Use

The interface should be intuitive and user-friendly. Consider whether your team will need extensive training to use the software effectively. Look for solutions with good customer support and documentation.

Scalability

Choose software that can grow with your business. Consider factors like the number of users, check volume limits, and additional features that might become important as your business expands.

Check Supply Considerations

Security Features

Modern check stock should include multiple security features such as watermarks, chemical reactive inks, security warnings, and microprinting. These features help prevent check washing, alteration, and counterfeiting.

Printer Compatibility

Ensure the check stock is compatible with your printer type (laser or inkjet) and that the paper weight and finish work well with your specific printer model. Some security features may not work well with certain printer types.

Bank Requirements

Check that your chosen supplies meet banking industry standards for paper weight, MICR line placement, and overall dimensions. Non-standard checks may be rejected by banks or cause processing delays.

Cost Considerations

While security is important, balance the cost of premium check stock against your actual risk level. High-volume users might benefit from bulk purchasing, while occasional users might prefer smaller quantities despite higher per-unit costs.

MICR Requirements

If you plan to print checks in-house, you'll need MICR (Magnetic Ink Character Recognition) capabilities. This might mean purchasing MICR toner cartridges for laser printers or pre-printed check stock with MICR encoding already applied.

Volume and Frequency

Consider how many checks you write monthly and whether you need batch processing capabilities. High-volume users should prioritize efficiency features like batch printing, while occasional users might prefer simpler, more cost-effective solutions.

Backup and Recovery

Ensure your chosen software solution includes robust backup capabilities and data recovery options. Cloud-based solutions often provide automatic backups, while desktop software may require manual backup procedures.

Compliance and Reporting

For businesses, consider whether the software provides adequate reporting for tax purposes, audit trails for compliance, and integration with existing financial reporting systems.

The right combination of check writing software and supplies depends on your specific needs, volume requirements, and security concerns. Whether you choose a comprehensive accounting solution like QuickBooks or a specialized tool like VersaCheck, ensure that your selection provides the security, functionality, and cost-effectiveness your situation requires. Remember that the initial cost is just one factor – consider ongoing costs, time savings, and security benefits when making your decision.
