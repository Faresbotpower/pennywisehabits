Buying Guide: How to Choose the Best Money Belt

Key Factors to Consider

Comfort and Materials: Since you'll potentially wear your money belt for 12+ hours, material choice is crucial. Silk offers the ultimate in comfort and moisture-wicking but costs more. Cotton blends provide good comfort at moderate prices, while synthetic materials offer durability and easy care at budget prices. Consider your skin sensitivity and typical travel climate when choosing.

Security Features: Modern money belts should include RFID blocking to prevent digital pickpocketing of contactless cards and passports. Look for quality zippers with fabric or rubber pulls that operate quietly. Some premium models include slash-resistant straps, though this may be overkill for most destinations.

Size and Capacity: Consider what you actually need to carry. At minimum, you should have space for one credit card, some emergency cash, and a photocopy of your passport. Avoid the temptation to choose oversized belts that encourage carrying non-essential items, making the belt bulky and obvious.

Adjustability: Look for belts that adjust to fit waist sizes at least 4-6 inches above and below your normal measurement to account for clothing layers and potential size changes during extended travel. Elastic bands offer comfort but may stretch out, while webbing straps provide durability but less flexibility.

What to Carry in Your Money Belt

Your money belt should contain only true emergencies items:

  • One backup credit card (different from your main wallet)
  • Enough cash for 1-2 days of basic expenses
  • Photocopy of your passport ID page
  • Emergency contact information
  • Critical medical information if applicable

Avoid carrying your primary cards, large amounts of cash, or anything you might need frequent access to throughout the day.

Proper Wearing and Maintenance

Position your money belt so the pouch sits on your hip or lower back rather than directly in front, which can create an obvious bulge when sitting. The belt should fit snugly but not restrictively—you should be able to slide one finger under the band.

Most money belts are machine washable, but check the care instructions. Silk models may require hand washing or gentle cycles. Always air dry to prevent shrinkage and maintain elastic properties.

When Money Belts Aren't Enough

While money belts are excellent for passive security, they're not suitable for all travel situations. In very high-risk areas, consider additional measures like dummy wallets, hotel safes, or distributing valuables across multiple hiding spots. Remember that money belts work best as part of a comprehensive travel security strategy.

The key to effective money belt use is selecting one that matches your specific travel style, comfort preferences, and security needs. Whether you choose our top-rated Eagle Creek Silk Undercover Money Belt or opt for a budget-friendly alternative, the most important factor is that you'll actually wear it consistently throughout your travels.
